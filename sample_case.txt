CASE NUMBER: 2024-CR-005678
PLAINTIFF: People of the State
DEFENDANT: Marcus Reynolds, James Tyler
COURT: County Criminal Court
JUDGE: Hon. Robert Mitchell
DATE FILED: 2024-02-20
LOCATION: County Courthouse, District 3
CHARGES: Theft, Fraud, Conspiracy
NARRATIVE:
On February 15, 2024, a residential burglary and theft were reported by Ms. Patricia Williams 
at 2847 Oak Street. Ms. Williams reported that approximately $8,500 in cash and jewelry valued 
at $12,000 were stolen from her home. Security camera footage showed two individuals, later 
identified as Marcus Reynolds (age 32) and James Tyler (age 29), entering the property at 
approximately 11:30 PM on February 14th. Both men wore dark clothing and had their faces obscured. 
The theft took approximately 23 minutes, after which they fled in a white sedan.

Investigation revealed that Marcus Reynolds had worked as a handyman at Ms. Williams' property 
two weeks prior, during which time he had access to the home and observed the locations of 
valuables. Phone records show contact between Reynolds and Tyler starting one week before the incident. 

On February 17th, law enforcement executed a search warrant at Reynolds' residence and recovered 
$3,200 in cash and two pieces of jewelry matching the victim's description. Additional evidence 
included blueprints of Ms. Williams' home with markings indicating entry points and locations 
of valuables.

James Tyler was apprehended attempting to pawn a diamond necklace belonging to the victim on 
February 18th. He initially claimed he purchased the necklace at a local shop but could not 
produce a receipt or identify the shop. Later investigation confirmed no jewelry shop in the 
area had any record of such a sale.

Both defendants were arrested and charged with theft (value over $10,000), fraud, and conspiracy 
to commit burglary.
